Frequently Asked Questions

+

How long will it take to build my website?

We aim to be realistic and transparent about timing. A simple informational site typically takes 5–8 weeks from kickoff through design, development, and testing, while an e-commerce or complex site can take around 8–12 weeks. We break the project into clear phases (planning, design concepts, development, review) and will give you an estimated schedule upfront. Throughout the process, we keep you updated on progress and adjust the timeline if your scope changes, so you always know what to expect.

+

What will my website project cost?

Every project is unique, so we provide a custom quote based on your requirements. In general, website costs can vary widely – basic sites might start in the low hundreds, while larger or highly customized sites run into the thousands. Key factors include the number of pages, design complexity, special features (e.g. e-commerce or integrations), and whether you need copywriting or graphics. We’ll discuss your needs in detail and then outline everything in a transparent proposal, so you know exactly what’s included (and what isn’t) – no surprises or hidden fees.

+

How many revisions are included?

We value your feedback and build in review rounds to get everything right. Typically, our standard process includes 2–3 rounds of revisions at key milestones. That means after delivering initial designs or mockups, you’ll have a chance to request changes (and we will carefully apply them). This approach strikes a balance: it gives you multiple opportunities to refine the site, while keeping the project on schedule. If extra changes are needed beyond the included rounds, we’re happy to make them – these are simply handled as agreed extras. We clearly document this revision policy up front so there are no misunderstandings, and everything stays on track.

+

What technology or platform will you use for my website?

We choose the tech stack based on what best fits your project and goals. We often work with widely used platforms like WordPress (popular for flexible content sites) or e-commerce solutions like Shopify/WooCommerce. For more custom applications we might use frameworks such as PHP/Laravel, Node.js, or modern JavaScript front-end libraries (React/Vue). As one example, many sites are built on WordPress because it powers nearly half of the web – and it’s listed among the most popular platforms along with PHP/Laravel, Drupal, Shopify, etc. During our kickoff call we’ll recommend the best tools for your needs, and of course we’ll explain our choice so you feel confident about the technology behind your site.

+

What about support and maintenance after launch?

Launch is just the beginning, and we’ll be there to help afterward. We include an initial period of post-launch support (e.g. the first 15 days) to fix any unforeseen bugs or minor tweaks. Beyond that, we recommend ongoing maintenance plans. Regular updates (to plugins, themes, or the CMS), security patches, backups, and minor enhancements are important – even “perfect” sites need checkups. In fact, web development experts advise monthly or quarterly maintenance plans to keep sites secure and running smoothly. Our maintenance services typically cover things like bug fixes, performance optimization, security updates, backups, and small feature updates. This way, you can focus on your business, and we make sure your site stays fresh and functional over time.
